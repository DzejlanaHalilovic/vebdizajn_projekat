What exactly is stem

What exactly is stem

That is, Science (it is important to emphasize that in English all natural sciences, physics, chemistry, biology… are united under this term), Engineering (all forms of engineering disciplines civil engineering, electrical engineering, architecture,…), Technology and Mathematics. Since this manual is intended for educators in preschool institutions, who have dedicated most of their education to social sciences and humanities, we must somehow bring closer the terms that are characteristic of the natural sciences, to understand and facilitate the entry of children and their educators into the wonderful world of natural sciences.

WHAT PEOPLE SAY ABOUT STEM

We always take into account the motivational words of people and their thoughts on creating a better world.

1

"Art is already a vital part of science, technology, engineering and math." “Art and design have played a prominent role in STEM, whether it’s pointing out to people or making notes,” says Liz Heinecke, author of several STEAM project books for children. "STEM lessons naturally include art, language arts and history."

2

"Art shows children how to creatively solve problems and value mistakes." Mental arithmetic and the ability to solve equations are important skills that engineers must possess, but creative approaches to problem solving really make a difference. Madeleine Burry says yes "Art gives children a place where they can make interesting mistakes and challenge themselves, especially because there is not always the right answer in art."

3

"Art reduces the intimidation factor of STEM and helps raise informed children." "Not every child has to be a scientist or an engineer, but the more you embrace the STEM discipline, the better citizen you will be," says Heinecke. "The more informed people are as children, the more they will grow into well-informed adults."
